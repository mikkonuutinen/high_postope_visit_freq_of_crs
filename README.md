# Age, co-morbidities and infections are associated with high postoperative visit frequency of chronic rhinosinusitis: a retrospective cluster study
This is a hub to host the research code of the manuscript: Age, co-morbidities and infections are associated with high postoperative visit frequency of chronic rhinosinusitis: a retrospective cluster study

### Objective
To identify reasons and patient characteristics associated with high postoperative visit frequency after endoscopic sinus surgery (ESS) for chronic rhinosinusitis (CRS)

### Methods
This retrospective cohort study included 767 CRS patients undergoing ESS in 2005-2019 at a tertiary referral center. High postoperative visit frequency was defined as >2 ENT outpatient visits within 6 months postoperatively. Data were extracted from electronic health records. Associations were assessed using age- and sex-adjusted logistic regression, and cluster analysis was used to identify patient subgroups.

### Results
High postoperative visit frequency occurred in 200 patients (26%). It was associated with infection-, pain-, and bleeding-related visits, older age, female sex, higher comorbidity burden, and conditions including asthma, allergy, chronic pulmonary disease. Cluster analysis identified six clusters; one predominantly female cluster had increased early postoperative visits and higher prevalence of eosinophilia and NSAID exacerbated respiratory disease.

### Conclusions
One-quarter of CRS patients experience increased postoperative healthcare use following ESS. Older age, female sex, comorbidities and type 2-associated inflammation are linked to higher visit frequency. Identifying high-risk patients may improve preoperative counseling and postoperative care planning.
